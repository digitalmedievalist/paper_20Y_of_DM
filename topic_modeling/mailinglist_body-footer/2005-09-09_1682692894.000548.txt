*Announcement: Residential fellowship available for scholar displaced by=20
Hurricane Katrina*With the support of the College of Arts & Sciences and the University of=20
Kentucky Libraries, the newly reorganized Collaboratory for Research in=20
Computing for Humanities (RCH) at the University of Kentucky,=20
Lexington,  is pleased to be able to offer an immediate residential=20
fellowship available to any one faculty member or ABD doctoral candidate=20
at an institution closed by Hurricane Katrina.As described in our new mission statement, RCH brings together faculty=20
and students from Engineering and the Humanities for research projects=20
with benefits for all involved. We provide infrastructure, technical=20
assistance, and grant writing assistance to individuals and groups who=20
propose projects under its auspices. We also encourage and support=20
interdisciplinary projects among individuals and groups from UK and=20
around the world.=20We invite proposals from individual scholars who are currently working=20
on a humanities computing project and require facilities to continue=20
work OR who are in the process of starting a new project and require=20
facilities and other assistance in getting the project off the ground.=20
Projects through RCH have traditionally focused on image-based editing=20
of medieval and, more recently, classical materials, however we are=20
currently in the exciting process of branching out and investigating new=20
topics. Any project focused on the electronic editing of or access to=20
humanistic materials (manuscripts, rare books, artworks) would be=20
acceptable.For more details on RCH, please visit our website at=20
http://www.rch.uky.edu/.=20The fellowship includes:   * Private workspace, including both PC and Macintosh workstations.
   * Access to our own hardware and software, and that of the
     Preservation and Digital Programs Division
     <http://www.uky.edu/Libraries/Special/> of the University of
     Kentucky Libraries (a list of hardware available at
     http://www.rch.uky.edu/index-prop.html#Fac)
   * An opportunity to speak through the "Wednesday Seminar" series at
     the Center for Computational Sciences <http://www.ccs.uky.edu/>.
   * Access to the RCH and Stoa.org <http://www.stoa.org> development
     and production servers. This includes online publication space, as
     well as archival storage space.
   * Consulting on issues of digital scholarship.Although we will probably not be able to offer a stipend, we are able to=20
provide some funding for relocation and assistance with a search for=20
housing.To apply, please send a letter of inquiry describing your project, a CV,=20
and contact information for three references. Please send application=20
materials by email to Dot Porter, the RCH Program Coordinator, at=20
dporter(a)uky.edu <mailto:dporter(a)uky.edu>, or by regular mail to:   Dot Porter
