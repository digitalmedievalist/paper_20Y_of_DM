>The Junius 11 CD-ROM (Bodleian Digital Texts 1) was published by=20
>the Bodleian rather than OUP. The Bodleian supported the project=20
>financially (the development of software), provided the images and=20
>contributed to the development of the interface. All the technical and=20
>academic work was done by Burnard Muir and his team. The images published=20
>on the CD were already available when the work on the project started six=20
>years ago: they are still available free of charge for personal use by=20
>researchers via Early Manuscripts at Oxford University=20
>(http://image.ox.ac.uk/). The next project in the series will be MS. Auct.=20
>F. 2.13 (Terence's Comedies). The images are also available via the Early=20
>Manuscripts at Oxford University site. The CD-ROM works only with Internet=20
>Explorer because Microsoft's implementation of JavaScript was used (you may =>have noticed the use of JavaScript in the extract posted by Martin). As far =>as I know Burnard Muir's team will try to overcome this limitation in the=20
>next CD-ROM.
