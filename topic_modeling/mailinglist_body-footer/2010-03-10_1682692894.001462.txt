> A Day in the Life of the Digital Humanities (Day of DH) is a community=20
> publication project that will bring together digital humanists from=20
> around the world to document what they do on one day, March 18th. The=20
> goal of the project is to create a web site that weaves together the=20
> journals of the participants into a picture that answers the question,=20
> =E2=80=9CJust what do computing humanists really do?=E2=80=9D Participants =
will=20
> document their day through photographs and commentary in a blog-like=20
> journal. The collection of these journals with links, tags, and=20
> comments will make up the final work which will be published online.
>
> On March 18th, participants will document and share the events of=20
> their day. However participants will also become co-authors, and the=20
> direction of the entire project will be influenced by their choices,=20
> both before and after the day of documentation. Eventually, the data=20
> will be grouped together, undergo some light semantic editing, and=20
> released for others to study. We hope that, beyond the original online=20
> publication, the raw data will be of use to those interested in=20
> further visualization or ethnographic experiments.
>
>
> A reminder to those who want to participate in the Day of Digital=20
> Humanities 2010 that we would appreciate you registering by the end of=20
> the 10th of March. We will be creating the blogs on the 11th so you=20
> have time to experiment.
>
> To find out about this project and to register go to:
>
> *http://tapor.ualberta.ca/taporwiki/index.php/Day_in_the_Life_of_the_Digita=
l_Humanities_2010
> *
