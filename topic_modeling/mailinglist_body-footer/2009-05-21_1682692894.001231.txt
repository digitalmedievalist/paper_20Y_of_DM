    Call for Bids: TEI Conference and Members Meeting, 2010The annual TEI Conference and Members' Meeting takes place every year in=20
late October or early November. We are now seeking bids to host this=20
event in 2010.The meeting this year (2009) will take place on November 11-15 at the=20
University of Michigan in Ann Arbor, MI, USA. The previous meetings have=20
been:    * London, England, November 6-8, 2008. hosted by King's College London.
    * College Park (MD), USA, October 31-November 3, 2007. Hosted by the
      University of Maryland.
    * Victoria, Canada, October 27-28, 2006. Hosted by the University of
      Victoria.
    * Sophia, Bulgaria, October 28-29, 2005. Hosted by the Bulgarian
      Academy of Sciences.
    * Baltimore, USA, October 22-23, 2004. Hosted by Johns Hopkins
      University.
    * Nancy, France, November 7-8 2003. Hosted by ATILF.
    * Chicago, USA, October 11-12 2002. Hosted by the Newberry Library
      and Northwestern University.
    * Pisa, Italy, November 16-17 2001. Hosted by the University of Pisa.The site of the conference and meeting has typically alternated between=20
Europe and North America, but that is not a fixed rule. We welcome=20
proposals from other parts of the world, and in particular from areas=20
where new TEI communities are arising.This year's conference and meeting will be a four-day event (plus=20
pre-conference workshops), with approximately 120 attendees. The three=20
days of the main conference will include a mix of plenary lectures by=20
invited speakers, round-table discussions, and conference-style=20
sessions; there will also be a business meeting of the membership. The=20
fourth day (Sunday) will be a closed session for members of the Board.=20
Meetings of TEI Special Interest Groups (SIGs) may also scheduled for=20
this day. Future meetings should assume roughly this shape, although=20
there is considerable room for local initiative in consultation with the=20
Board.The TEI Consortium guarantees direct costs of the conference and meeting=20
up to a maximum of US$5200 with special provisions for funding=20
attendance in excess of approximately 120 attendees. The conference=20
organising committee is also expected to seek additional funds from=20
local institutions, commercial sponsors, and other organisations. A=20
conference registration fee is charged to assist the TEI in recovering=20
its expenditure and ensure that it is able to underwrite the cost of=20
future conferences.Bids should be sent to info(a)tei-c.org by no later than September 1,=20
2009, though institutions considering making a proposal are encouraged=20
to contact chair of the TEI (daniel.odonnell(a)uleth.ca=20
<mailto:daniel.odonnell(a)uleth.ca>) much earlier in the process in order=20
to discuss their ideas. Bids should include the following information:    * The name of the institution(s) making the bid
    * The name, address, email, and telephone number of the contact person
    * A brief description of the facilities available for the event
      (rooms, equipment, technical support, food)
    * An indication of what financial support, if any, the hosting
      institution is prepared to give (for instance, sponsoring one or
      more receptions or pre-meeting workshops; payment of travel
      expenses for one or more plenary speakers; etc.)
    * Any other details that may be useful in assessing the bid (e.g.
      the presence of a conference on a related topic at the institution
      around the time of the meeting; the launch of a new TEI-related
      initiative at the institution, etc., ideas for a particular theme
      or focus).In submitting bids, local organisers are encouraged to be creative: the=20
TEI is willing to work with hosts to reflect local interests and strengths.Further information about the requirements for the conference and=20
members meeting may be found in our document on Hosting a Members=20
Meeting <http://www.tei-c.org/Membership/Meetings/meeting_hosting.xml>=20
and the Board's own Practices and Procedures=20
<http://www.tei-c.org/Board/procedures.xml#body.1_div.4> document.All bids will be reviewed by the TEI board, which makes the final decision.--=20
