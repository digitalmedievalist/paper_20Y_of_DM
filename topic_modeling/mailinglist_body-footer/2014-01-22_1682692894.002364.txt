DiXiT Camp 1
28 April =E2=80=93 2 May 2014, Cambridge & LondonWe are very pleased to announce the fifth year of this course, funded=20
by the Digital Scholarly Editions Initial Training Network (DiXiT),  and=20
run by DiXiT with the Institute of English Studies (London), the=20
University of Cambridge, the Warburg Institute, and King's College=20
London. For the first time, the course will run in two parallel=20
strands: one on medieval and the other on modern manuscripts.The course is open to any arts and humanities doctoral students  working=20
with manuscripts. It involves five days of intensive training  on the=20
analysis, description and editing of medieval or modern  manuscripts to=20
be held jointly in Cambridge and London. Participants  will receive a=20
solid theoretical foundation and hands-on experience in  cataloguing and=20
editing manuscripts for both print and digital formats.The first half of the course involves morning classes and then=20
afternoon visits to libraries in Cambridge and London. Participants=20
will view original manuscripts and gain practical experience in=20
applying the morning's themes to concrete examples. In the second hal=
f=20
we will address the cataloguing and description of manuscripts in a=20
digital format with particular emphasis on the Text Encoding  Initiative=20
(TEI). These sessions will also combine theoretical  principles and=20
practical experience and include supervised work on  computers.The course is free of charge but is open only to doctoral students  (PhD=20
or equivalent). It is aimed at those writing dissertations  relating to=20
medieval or modern manuscripts, especially those working  on literature,=20
art or history. Some bursaries will be available for  travel and=20
accommodation. There are eighteen vacancies across the  medieval and=20
modern strands, and preference will be given to those  considered by the=20
selection panel likely to benefit most from the  course. Applications=20
close on 14 February 2014 but early registration  is strongly recommended.For further details see http://dixit.uni-koeln.de/mmsda.html
