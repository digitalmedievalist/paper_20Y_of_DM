welcome occasion to share our knowledge of indices, tools and strategies=20
to find original medieval texts provided on the web (digital libraries,=20
corpora, editions, etc.).Francesco Stella pointed to the following useful lists:  * "How do I find Electronic Full-Text Sources (EFTS)", University
    Library of Chicago:
    https://guides.lib.uchicago.edu/efts/medieval
  * "Medieval Texts, Literature and Languages", Medieval Study Programs,
    University of British Columbia, e.g. Latin:
    https://medieval.arts.ubc.ca/resources/online-resources/medieval-texts-li=
terature-and-languages/latin/
  * ...More catalogues and databases:  * DLL Catalog: https://catalog.digitallatin.org/
  * MIRABILE - Archivio digitale della cultura medievale:
    http://www.mirabileweb.it/
  * The Medieval Academy of America's "Curated Guide and Database" of
    medieval digital resources: http://mdr-maa.org
  * "Arturian Fiction in Medieval Europe":
    https://service.arthurianfiction.org
  * The DIMEV: An Open-Access, Digital Edition of the Index of Middle
    English Verse: https://www.dimev.net/
  * ...More archives and libraries  * Corpus Corporum: http://www.mlat.uzh.ch/MLS/
  * Archivio della Latinit=C3=A0 Italiana del Medioevo (ALIM):
    http://alim.unisi.it/
    (DM review:
    https://journal.digitalmedievalist.org/articles/10.16995/dm.79/)
  * ...Personally, I have no intention to systematically collect or evaluate=20
this kind of information. But I am eager to see what other colleagues=20
might suggest. And I hope this helps others to find what they are=20
looking for or to create and update respective indices, tools and=20
resources. This is what this mailing list is made for, to support and=20
encourage an open exchange.Best,
Franz
